How to Import Recipes Into Your Shopping List Automatically

Stop copying ingredients one by one. Here's how to import any recipe from any website directly into your shopping list in seconds.

You found a recipe you want to make this week. It looks good. The ingredients list is long — twelve items, some of which you're not sure you have at home.

So you open your grocery app and start typing. Chicken breast. Done. Heavy cream. Done. Sun-dried tomatoes. How do you spell that again? There's a better way.

The problem with manual ingredient entry

Most people either type their grocery list from scratch or copy ingredients one by one from a recipe website. Both take time and both introduce the risk of missing something or misspelling an item.

The gap between "I want to cook this" and "I have everything I need" is where meal planning usually breaks down.

How recipe import works in Basky

Basky lets you import recipes directly from any website. Copy the link, paste it into Basky, and the app pulls out every single ingredient automatically — with quantities in grams and millilitres.

No typing. No switching between apps. No forgotten items. The whole process takes about ten seconds.

Which recipe sites work?

Basky works with most popular recipe websites. If the recipe is published online with a standard ingredient list, Basky can import it. This includes major food blogs, cooking sites and recipe databases.

From recipe to ready-to-shop in seconds

Once the ingredients are imported, you can review them, deselect anything you already have at home and add the rest to your shopping list with one tap.

If you're cooking two recipes this week, import both. Your list builds itself.

Recipe import is included in Basky Pro — a one-time purchase.
