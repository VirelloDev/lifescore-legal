How to Share a Grocery List with Your Partner (And Actually Stay in Sync)

Tired of calling each other from the store? Here's how a shared grocery list keeps your whole household in sync in real time.

We've all been there. You're standing in the dairy aisle, staring at the yoghurt section, and your phone rings. It's your partner asking if you already picked up milk. You hadn't. They had. You both bought milk.

Shared grocery shopping sounds simple in theory. In practice, it's a constant loop of missed calls, duplicate purchases and forgotten items. The problem isn't communication — it's that most grocery lists aren't built for two people.

Why a shared list needs to be live

A static list — whether it's a notes app, a text message or a piece of paper — stops being accurate the moment someone starts shopping. Items get checked off on one person's phone but not the other's. Someone adds an item after the other person has already passed that aisle.

For a shared list to actually work, it needs to update instantly for everyone. Not after a sync. Not after a refresh. Instantly.

How Basky handles shared lists

Basky lets you share a list with your partner, family or housemate via a simple link. When someone checks off an item, everyone sees it immediately. When someone adds something, it appears on every device straight away.

There are no merge conflicts, no version history to manage and no calls from the dairy aisle.

Setting it up takes less than a minute

Create a list, tap the share button and send the link. Your partner opens the link and they're looking at the same list as you — live.

You can share as many lists as you want. One for the weekly groceries, one for the dinner party on Saturday, one for the things you need from the hardware store.

One-time purchase, no subscription

Basky Pro — which includes household sharing — is a one-time purchase. No monthly fee, no annual subscription. You pay once and it works forever.
